About Me

I’m a dedicated firefighter with a deep passion for serving my community and protecting what matters most. Off the front lines, I’ve developed a growing interest in web design — a creative outlet where I can build, problem-solve, and keep learning. Whether I’m responding to emergencies or exploring new lines of code, I bring the same drive, focus, and heart. Most of all, I’m a proud family person who believes in showing up fully — both at home and on the job.

Experience

As a firefighter with 20 years of experience, I’ve gained extensive knowledge and continuously worked to hone my craft. While I may not have as much experience in web design, I bring the same dedication and willingness to learn that has served me well in the fire service. I’ve completed a variety of courses to strengthen my programming skills, and I’m passionate about continuing to grow in this field.

As a fire service instructor, I believe this commitment to lifelong learning is essential—not only for personal growth but also to effectively teach and inspire others.

Certifications

Aside from holding a certificate in 1001, I hold various certifications including Fire Officer and Instructor. The learning doesn't stop - I am always looking to level up because the learning never stops in this trade.

Bio

In 2001, right after 9/11, I made the decision to join my local volunteer fire department. My father was a firefighter for about 20 years, so I thought "now" is a good time to carry the torch. I stayed on serving my community while working a fulltime job installing cable and Internet making a mediocre wage - enough to pay rent at least. That's when I started experimenting with web design, learning HTML and CSS. From there I progressed to other programming languages like Javascript and Visual Basic. I wasn't a huge fan of Visual Basic. I guess designing and programming computer programs wasn't my cup of tea so I stuck with web design.

After about seven years I decided I needed more in my life. Something with meaning instead of installing cable and Internet. Something just clicked and I knew what I had to do - join the Canadian Armed Forces (CAF). I did that for five years and consequently my wife decided it was time for me to stay home.
