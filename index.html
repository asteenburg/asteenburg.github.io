Bio

In 2001, right after 9/11, I made the decision to join my local volunteer fire department. My father was a firefighter for about 20 years, so I thought "now" is a good time to carry the torch. I stayed on serving my community while working a fulltime job installing cable and Internet making a mediocre wage - enough to pay rent at least. That's when I started experimenting with web design, learning HTML and CSS. From there I progressed to other programming languages like Javascript and Visual Basic. I wasn't a huge fan of Visual Basic. I guess designing and programming computer programs wasn't my cup of tea so I stuck with web design.

After about seven years I decided I needed more in my life. Something with meaning instead of installing cable and Internet. Something just clicked and I knew what I had to do - join the Canadian Armed Forces (CAF). I did that for five years and then it was time for me to stay home. Now, I am a aircraft firefighter at a major airport, but the funny thing is, I have always enjoyed dabbling with code. I will admit, I'm not very good at it and I have a long way to go. When I first started to learn Netscape was the browser of choice and Javascript was super simple. HTML was also very basic, and now there are so many different languages to use for not only building, but styling - CSS, Tailwind CSS, Bootstrap, the list goes on.
