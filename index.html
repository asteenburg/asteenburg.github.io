Bio

In 2001, right after 9/11, I joined my local volunteer fire department. My father was a firefighter for about 20 years, so I thought "now" is a good time to carry the torch. I stayed on serving my community while working full-time installing cable and Internet. That's when I started experimenting with web design, learning HTML and CSS. From there, I progressed to other programming languages like Javascript and Visual Basic. I wasn't a huge fan of Visual Basic, so I stuck with web design.

After about seven years, I decided I needed more in my life. Something with meaning instead of installing cable and Internet. I joined the Canadian Armed Forces (CAF) for five years and then transitioned to become an aircraft firefighter at a major airport. I have always enjoyed dabbling with code, though I am still learning and improving my skills in web design.
